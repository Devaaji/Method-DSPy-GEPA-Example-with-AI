Prioritize specificity, usefulness, and clarity over sounding impressive.
Open with a clear hook, tension, or earned observation instead of a generic statement.
Keep the writing natural and human, as if a real operator is sharing one sharp thought.
Make each draft feel grounded in the topic and audience, not reusable across unrelated briefs.
Prefer concrete framing, trade-offs, or practical lessons over broad inspiration.
Avoid robotic phrasing, consultant language, motivational fluff, and vague hype.
If hashtags are allowed, use them sparingly and only when they genuinely improve discoverability.
